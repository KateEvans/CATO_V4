File your Company Tax Return with HMRC

You can use HM Revenue and Customs' (HMRC's) online service to file your:

  • Company Tax Return with HMRC
  • annual accounts with Companies House (if you're a limited company)

You'll need a set of accounts with figures rounded to whole numbers. It's easier to complete your filing if you prepare accounts in this format.

You'll also need details of any:

  • director’s loans (money owed to your business) that weren’t repaid by the end of the tax period you're filing for
  • capital allowances you’re claiming
  • profit you made from selling assets (‘chargeable gains’)
  • reliefs you’re claiming

Accessing the service

To use the service, you'll need:

  • a Government Gateway user ID and password - register for an HMRC online account if you don’t have one
  • Companies House password and authentication code if you’re filing accounts with Companies House - register with Companies House if you haven’t already

Paper returns

You can only file a paper tax return if one of the following applies:

  • your company is insolvent and is being run by an administrator
  • a court has ordered your company to be ‘wound up’
  • everyone running your company is prevented from using a computer on religious grounds

Last updated: 25 February 2015.
