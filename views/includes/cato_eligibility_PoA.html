Do you want to change the dates of your reporting periods?

You can change your company’s reporting period (its financial year). You could do this to line up with other important dates, for example.

Companies House and HM Revenue and Customs (HMRC) have different reporting periods for every company. They need to align.


HMRC period 1: 1 December 2013 to 30 November 2014
HMRC period 2: 1 December 2013 to 1 May 2015

Your reporting period for Companies House is longer than 12 months, so you have 2 HMRC periods to fill the gap.

Why change the reporting period dates?

You can change the end date of your Companies House period (‘period of accounts’) by 7 days. This will make your Companies House period longer or shorter.

You could do this to align the Companies House reporting period with other important dates, for example.

If you change your Companies House period, you’ll need to change your HMRC period so the start and end dates match.

Your HMRC reporting period can be a maximum of 12 months. So if your Companies House period is longer than 12 months, you’ll need an extra HMRC period to fill the gap.
