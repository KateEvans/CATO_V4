You can’t use this service if you need to report the following things in your accounts:

  • a gain or loss from a capital asset, like an investment (but you can report gains and losses on assets you use for your business)
  • transactions and income from outside the UK
  • adjustments for something you reported in a previous year
  • financial instruments - a contract or binding agreement to buy or sell something
  • costs of restructuring - for example, costs of laying off staff or closing down an office
Do you need to report any of these?
  • You can't file to HM Revenue and Customs with this service You can still use this service to file with Companies House. To file to HMRC, you can either:
    • download filing software in PDF format (check that you're eligible to use it at the beginning of the PDF)
    • use accounting software
Do you need to report income or expenditure from shares?

You can use this service to report dividends, but you can't report the following types of income or expenditure from shares:

  • called up share capital not paid - the amount still due from people who've bought shares
  • share-based payments - payments for employees if they've been given a share of the company
  • share premium - the difference between a share's price (printed on the certificate) and how much it sold for
Do you need to report these types of assets?

You can’t use this service to report the following types of assets:

  • contingent assets - assets or income you're expecting but can't predict at the moment (for example, the outcome of a court case)
  • investment assets - for example, property or stocks and shares
  • profits or loss from selling fixed (physical) assets
